\begin{abstract}
Detectors that threshold a globally scaled gradient statistic suffer from
\emph{precision collapse}: as broadband noise increases, false detections
accumulate faster than any global threshold can adapt, rendering event catalogs
unreliable at high turbulence.
Locally normalized detectors such as PVI partially compensate, but retain a
residual false-discovery fraction tied to their local variance estimate.
We introduce MASS/SMASH, a two-stage change-point detector for sign-flip
events combining an \emph{antipodal correlation pre-filter}---which scores
candidate positions for sign-reversal antisymmetry rather than raw gradient
magnitude---with a Minimum Description Length (MDL) gate whose acceptance
criterion is denominated in bits of compression gain rather than any
signal-derived statistic.

We validate MASS/SMASH on three synthetic solar wind benchmarks.
In a single-regime experiment (30 days, 120 injected crossings, 30\%
Kolmogorov turbulence), MASS/SMASH achieves precision 0.921 and recall 0.483,
contributing 24 true events missed by PVI; a joint catalog covers 95\% of
injected crossings at $\approx$5\% combined false-discovery fraction.
An SNR sweep across turbulence amplitudes 5\%--50\% of the background field,
run over 5 independent random seeds, reveals a distinctive pattern:
MASS/SMASH mean precision \emph{rises} from $0.853\pm0.099$ at 5\% turbulence
to $0.937\pm0.047$ at 50\%---increasing selectivity as noise grows---while
the $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$ threshold baseline falls from precision 1.000 to
0.035 over the same range.
PVI precision is stable at 0.967--0.968 across all levels.
PELT \citep{Killick2012} with the BIC penalty produces more than 1{,}600
detections for 28 true events at every turbulence level tested ($P \approx
0.017$), demonstrating that signal-derived penalties are overwhelmed by
Kolmogorov noise irrespective of amplitude; even at $1{,}000\times$ BIC,
precision reaches only 0.084.
A contaminated benchmark with 40 non-antipodal confounding events shows the
baseline fires on 98\% of confounders, MASS/SMASH on 18\%, and PVI on 8\%.

A 20-day pilot on real Wind/MFI data (NASA CDAWeb) yields 154 MASS/SMASH
detections (7.7 day$^{-1}$) versus 1{,}197 for the baseline and 2{,}397 for
PVI, consistent with the high-turbulence synthetic pattern.
We introduce \emph{projective PVI} ($\pPVI$), replacing the Euclidean
increment with geodesic distance in $\mathbb{RP}^2$ to isolate pure
field-direction changes; $\pPVI$ achieves recall 0.964--1.000 on
polarity-reversal events, defining a precision-recall complementarity with
MASS/SMASH.
\end{abstract}

\textbf{Keywords:} change-point detection; sign-flip; antipodal correlation;
Minimum Description Length; precision stability; solar wind; current sheets;
partial variance of increments; projective geometry; structural break;
information theory

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{The Precision-Stability Problem in Sign-Flip Detection}

Many physical and engineered systems produce time series that undergo
\emph{sign-flip change-points}: abrupt transitions from a steady state near
$+\mu_0$ to an equal-magnitude negative state near $-\mu_0$, embedded in
broadband noise.
In the solar wind, magnetic field polarity reversals at sector boundaries and
current sheet crossings exhibit this structure in the radial or out-of-ecliptic
field components.
Sign flips appear in EEG (reference electrode inversions, certain seizure
complexes), climate indices (ENSO polarity transitions), and AC power systems
(line-to-line faults, inverter failures).

A shared failure mode across these domains is \emph{precision collapse}.
Detectors that threshold a globally scaled, non-normalized gradient statistic
fire on any large local excursion; as noise amplitude increases, spurious
detections accumulate faster than the global threshold can adapt.
A detector firing at $\mu + k\sigma$ of the \emph{global} gradient
distribution achieves perfect recall but its false discovery fraction (FDF)
grows monotonically with turbulence because the global distribution develops
heavier tails.
This failure is specific to globally normalized statistics; locally normalized
detectors such as PVI compensate partially by tracking local variance and are
indeed more stable, as our experiments confirm (Section~\ref{subsec:snr}).
Figure~\ref{fig:snr_sweep} quantifies the collapse: the $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$
baseline falls from precision 1.000 at 5\% turbulence to 0.035 at 50\%
(mean across 5 seeds, 28 true events per experiment).

Even signal-adaptive change-point methods are vulnerable.
PELT \citep{Killick2012} with its standard BIC penalty
$\beta = \log(N)\hat{\sigma}^2$ scales the penalty with estimated noise
variance, but this estimate is itself overwhelmed when Kolmogorov turbulence
produces thousands of short-duration mean offsets whose combined BIC penalty
score exceeds that of a genuine structural break.
In our experiments PELT produces more than 1{,}600 detections for 28 true
events at \emph{every} turbulence level from 5\% to 50\%---essentially
no precision behavior at all (Section~\ref{subsec:snr}).

\subsection{MASS/SMASH: A Conservative Complementary Selector}

We introduce MASS/SMASH (Multi-seAm Seam-Sensitive Modeling /
Symmetric Minimum-description-length Antipodal Seam Hunting), whose design
goal is not to maximize recall, but to provide a conservative, structurally
selective complement to gradient-based detectors: a method that contributes
non-redundant true detections while maintaining low FDF across a wide noise
range.

Two design choices underpin this goal.

\textbf{Structural pre-filtering via antipodal correlation.}
Each candidate position $\tau$ is scored for antisymmetry between the pre-
and post-window signals:
$\mathcal{A}(\tau) = \mathrm{corr}(y[\tau-w:\tau],\,-\,y[\tau:\tau+w])$.
Only positions with $\mathcal{A}(\tau) > 0.35$ advance to the MDL stage.
A turbulent spike with random phase structure scores near zero on $\mathcal{A}$
regardless of amplitude; a genuine sign-flip scores near 1.0.
This pre-filter rejects structural confounders that gradient detectors accept.

\textbf{MDL gain threshold denominated in bits.}
The acceptance criterion requires a compression gain of $G = 20$ bits---a
fixed information-theoretic cost, not a multiple of any signal-derived
statistic.
As turbulence increases, both single- and two-segment model residuals grow, but
the systematic residual reduction from a true structural break grows
proportionally; for noise-only fluctuations no such systematic reduction
exists regardless of amplitude (Section~\ref{subsec:stability}).
The empirical consequence is that MASS/SMASH precision remains high and even
\emph{increases} from SNR 20:1 down to SNR 2:1 on this benchmark, as the
absolute threshold becomes increasingly selective relative to the growing
noise floor that saturates the baseline.

\subsection{Solar Wind as a Validation Domain}

We demonstrate MASS/SMASH on solar wind current sheet detection, a
well-studied instance of the sign-flip detection problem.
The domain provides a good benchmark: the sign-flip structure is
well-characterized, a high-performance comparison method (PVI) exists, and
synthetic ground truth is precisely recoverable.
A 20-day pilot on real Wind/MFI data provides a qualitative plausibility
check (Section~\ref{sec:real_data}).
Extensions to other domains are discussed in
Section~\ref{sec:applications}.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{Change-Point Detection}

Classical change-point detection addresses mean-shift, variance-shift,
and distributional-shift events \citep{Basseville1993,Truong2020}.
CUSUM \citep{Page1954} detects persistent mean offsets under sequential
testing; it requires two opposing statistics to handle sign reversals.
Binary segmentation \citep{Vostrikova1981} recursively splits the series at the
most significant change-point.
PELT \citep{Killick2012} solves the penalized segmentation problem
$\min_m \sum_{i=1}^{m+1} C(y_{t_{i-1}+1:t_i}) + \beta m$ in
$\mathcal{O}(n)$ time; standard cost functions target mean or variance fit
quality rather than antisymmetric structure.
Bayesian online change-point detection \citep{AdamsMacKay2007} maintains a
posterior over run-length, providing principled uncertainty quantification.

None of these methods directly addresses the precision-collapse problem of
Section~\ref{sec:intro}: their thresholds (likelihood ratios, posterior
probabilities, penalization coefficients) are derived from signal statistics
and are therefore susceptible to heavy-tailed noise.
We confirm this empirically in Section~\ref{subsec:snr}: PELT with the BIC
penalty produces more than 1{,}600 false detections for 28 true events across
turbulence levels 5\%--50\%, with essentially no dependence on amplitude.
MDL-based segmentation \citep{Rissanen1978,Davis2006} is the closest
prior approach: the $\log_2 N$-bit location penalty is independent of signal
amplitude.
MASS/SMASH extends MDL segmentation with an antisymmetry pre-filter
targeting sign-flip events specifically.

\subsection{Sign-Flip and Antipodal Structure}

We are not aware of prior change-point work that uses cross-window antisymmetry
(antipodal correlation) as the primary candidate generator.
Boundary detection in image processing uses mirrored filter responses
\citep{Canny1986}, applied to spatial edges rather than temporal sign-flip
transitions.
The related concept of antipodal symmetry has not, to our knowledge, been
applied to sequential change-point detection.

\subsection{Solar Wind Current Sheet Detection}

Automated current sheet detection in heliophysics uses primarily:
(1) the Partial Variance of Increments \citep[PVI;][]{Greco2008};
(2) directional discontinuity analysis using minimum variance or hodogram
criteria \citep{Neugebauer1984};
(3) wavelet decomposition for intermittency characterization \citep{Bruno2013}.
PVI has partial precision stability because its local variance normalization
partially compensates for turbulence; we quantify this in
Section~\ref{subsec:snr}.

\section{The MASS/SMASH Algorithm}
\label{sec:algorithm}
%% ============================================================

MASS/SMASH processes a univariate time series $y_1, \ldots, y_N$ and returns
a set of seam locations $\{\tau_1, \ldots, \tau_m\}$ with associated MDL
gain scores.
The pipeline has four stages: (1) candidate proposal from two detectors,
(2) non-maximum suppression (NMS), (3) per-configuration MDL scoring, and
(4) global MDL selection.
All components are domain-agnostic.

\subsection{Antipodal Correlation Detector}
\label{subsec:antipodal}

The antipodal correlation detector scores each candidate position $\tau$ by:
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{A}(\tau) \;=\; \mathrm{corr}\!\left(y[\tau-w:\tau],\;
    -\,y[\tau:\tau+w]\right),
  \label{eq:antipodal}
\end{equation}
where $w$ is the half-window size (default 20 samples) and both windows are
$z$-score normalized before computing the Pearson correlation.
The score $\mathcal{A}(\tau) \approx 1$ when the $w$ samples immediately
before $\tau$ are temporally coherent with the negated $w$ samples after
$\tau$: the defining signature of a sign-flip transition.
For turbulent fluctuations with random phase structure,
$\mathcal{A}(\tau) \approx 0$ regardless of amplitude.
The pre-filter selects for structure rather than for signal magnitude.

Equation~\eqref{eq:antipodal} uses forward-ordered windows, not true mirror
antisymmetry, which would require one window to be time-reversed.
For narrow sign-flip transitions (width $\ll w$), both formulas agree;
for wider transitions they differ.
Localization behavior is characterized empirically in
Section~\ref{subsec:offset}.
Candidates are local maxima of $\mathcal{A}$ above threshold 0.35, retained
after NMS with minimum separation 60 s.

\subsection{Roughness Discontinuity Detector}
\label{subsec:roughness}

The roughness at position $i$ is $\mathcal{R}(i) = \sigma(\Delta y[i:i+w_r])$,
where $\Delta y$ denotes first differences and $w_r$ is the roughness window
(default 20 samples).
The normalized roughness change
\begin{equation}
  \mathcal{D}(\tau) \;=\;
    \frac{|\mathcal{R}(\tau) - \mathcal{R}(\tau-1)|}{\sigma(\mathcal{R})}
  \label{eq:roughness}
\end{equation}
identifies positions where local variance changes discontinuously, including
the edges of wide or turbulence-embedded transition layers.
Candidates are local maxima of $\mathcal{D}$ above threshold 0.20, with NMS.

\subsection{MDL Model Selection}
\label{subsec:mdl}

After merging the top-$k^*$ candidates from both detectors (default $k^*=3$),
MASS/SMASH scores all seam configurations with the MDL objective:
\begin{equation}
  \MDL(m, \hat{\boldsymbol{\theta}}) \;=\;
    \underbrace{\frac{N}{2}\log_2\frac{\RSS}{N}}_{\text{data fit}}
    \;+\;
    \underbrace{\frac{p}{2}\log_2 N}_{\text{model complexity}}
    \;+\;
    \underbrace{\frac{\alpha}{2}\,m\,\log_2 N}_{\text{seam encoding}},
  \label{eq:mdl}
\end{equation}
where $\RSS$ is the pooled residual sum of squares across segments, $p$ is
the total model parameter count, $m$ is the number of seams, and $\alpha = 2.0$.
The seam encoding term reflects the $\approx m\log_2 N$-bit cost of
specifying seam locations.
A no-seam configuration ($m=0$) is always included; a detection requires an
explicit positive MDL gain over the single-segment model.

Within each segment, MASS/SMASH evaluates a model zoo: Fourier series at
$K = 4, 8, 12$ harmonics, polynomials of degree 2, 3, and 5, and
autoregressive models of orders 5, 10, and 15.
The best model per segment is selected by BIC contribution.

\subsection{Mechanism for Empirical Precision Stability}
\label{subsec:stability}

The MDL gain from introducing one seam is:
\begin{equation}
  \Delta\MDL \;=\; \MDL_0 - \MDL_1 \;\approx\;
    \frac{N}{2}\log_2\frac{\RSS_0}{\RSS_1} - \frac{\alpha}{2}\log_2 N,
  \label{eq:gain}
\end{equation}
where $\RSS_0$ and $\RSS_1$ are the single- and two-segment residual sums
of squares.

For a window of $N$ samples containing a clean sign-flip from $+A$ to $-A$
at the midpoint, embedded in i.i.d.\ Gaussian noise with standard deviation
$\sigma_n$:
the single-segment model fits the mean near zero, accumulating
$\RSS_0 \approx N(A^2 + \sigma_n^2)$; the two-segment model fits each half
at its mean, leaving only noise, $\RSS_1 \approx N\sigma_n^2$.
The ratio is:
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\RSS_0}{\RSS_1} \;\approx\; 1 + \left(\frac{A}{\sigma_n}\right)^2,
  \label{eq:rss_ratio}
\end{equation}
so the MDL gain is $(N/2)\log_2(1 + (A/\sigma_n)^2) - (\alpha/2)\log_2 N$.
Strong crossings (large $A/\sigma_n$) accumulate sufficient gain even at
high turbulence; weak crossings where $A/\sigma_n$ is too small never reach
20 bits and are consistently missed.

For a turbulent fluctuation with no underlying structural break, the
best-found split does not systematically reduce the residual:
$\RSS_0/\RSS_1 \approx 1$ in expectation, and
$\Delta\MDL \approx -(\alpha/2)\log_2 N < 0$.
The seam-encoding penalty cancels the expected gain from the best of $N$
random splits \citep{Rissanen1978,Davis2006}, suppressing noise-driven
candidates regardless of their amplitude.
This is the qualitative mechanism underlying the precision stability observed
in Figure~\ref{fig:snr_sweep}.
We present it as such---a qualitative argument and empirical observation---not
a theorem; practical factors including windowing, model-zoo selection, and
cumulative-gain aggregation affect actual gain values.

The key contrast with the BIC penalty used in PELT is that the BIC penalty
scales as $\beta \propto \log(N)\hat{\sigma}^2$: it is \emph{proportional}
to the noise variance estimate.
When Kolmogorov turbulence generates thousands of short-duration local mean
offsets, $\hat{\sigma}^2$ underestimates the true structural variability and
the penalty is insufficient to suppress them.
The absolute 20-bit MDL threshold is immune to this mechanism because it does
not scale with signal statistics.

\subsection{Chunked Inference on Long Time Series}
\label{subsec:chunked}

For long time series, MASS/SMASH applies in a sliding-window fashion: windows
of length $W$ samples step $S < W$, with $m_\mathrm{max}=1$ seam per window.
Each sample accumulates a cumulative MDL gain across all windows in which it
was selected; a global NMS pass retains peaks above threshold $G$.
For the solar wind case study: $W = 200$ samples (600 s), $S = 100$ samples
(300 s), $G = 20$ bits.

\begin{algorithm}[t]
\caption{MASS/SMASH chunked inference}
\label{alg:chunked}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\Require Signal $y[0:N]$, window $W$, step $S$, gain threshold $G$
\State Initialize $\mathrm{gain}[0:N] \gets 0$
\For{$\mathrm{start} \in \{0, S, 2S, \ldots, N-W\}$}
  \State Run MASS/SMASH on $y[\mathrm{start}:\mathrm{start}+W]$ with $m_\mathrm{max}=1$
  \If{best solution has $m>0$ and MDL gain $g>0$}
    \For{each local seam index $\tau_\ell$}
      \State $\mathrm{gain}[\mathrm{start}+\tau_\ell] \mathrel{+}= g$
    \EndFor
  \EndIf
\EndFor
\State Retain positions with $\mathrm{gain} > G$; NMS with $\Delta\tau_\mathrm{min}$
\State \Return detected positions and scores
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}

\subsection{Component Ablation}
\label{subsec:ablation}

To attribute the precision-stability property to specific components, we run
four ablation variants on the SNR sweep (7 turbulence levels, 28 events,
seed 42):

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Antipodal-only}: antipodal candidates accepted directly,
    no MDL gate.
  \item \textbf{Roughness+MDL}: roughness candidates only, antipodal
    pre-filter disabled.
  \item \textbf{Antipodal+MDL}: antipodal candidates only, roughness

  \item \textbf{Full MASS/SMASH}: both candidate generators, MDL gate.
\end{enumerate}

Two findings emerge clearly from Table~\ref{tab:ablation}.

\textbf{The MDL gate is the primary source of precision stability.}
Antipodal-only produces 1,316 detections with precision 0.014 at every
turbulence level: the antisymmetry pre-filter alone provides no precision
stability.
All three MDL-gated variants maintain precision 1.000 for this single-seed
evaluation.

\textbf{The roughness detector is the primary driver of recall.}
At turbulence $\geq 10\%$, Roughness+MDL recall equals Full MASS/SMASH
recall exactly, while Antipodal+MDL recall is lower (0.393--0.536).
The roughness detector's candidates---transition-edge positions where local
variance changes---align better with the MDL gain peaks than do the antipodal
candidates at moderate and high turbulence.
At 5\% turbulence, Full MASS/SMASH recall (0.500) exceeds Roughness+MDL
(0.429), showing that antipodal candidates contribute additional true
detections only at the highest SNR.

This decomposition clarifies the mechanism claim: precision stability derives
from the \emph{MDL gate}, not the antipodal pre-filter.
The roughness detector provides recall; the MDL gate provides selectivity;
the antipodal pre-filter provides marginal recall benefit at low turbulence
and structural selectivity for confounder rejection
(Section~\ref{subsec:contaminated}).

\section{Case Study Methodology: Solar Wind Current Sheets}
\label{sec:casestudy}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{Synthetic Solar Wind Data}
\label{subsec:synth}

We validate MASS/SMASH on synthetic magnetic field time series calibrated to
mimic Wind/MFI observations near solar maximum.
The background field is $\hat{\mathbf{b}}_0 = (0.201, 0.201, 0.958)$
(normalized), giving $B_0 = 6\nT$ with $\approx 96\%$ of the reversal
amplitude in $B_z$.
Polarity reversals are modeled as:
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{pol}(t) \;=\; \tanh\!\left(\frac{t - t_\mathrm{cross}}{\delta/3}\right),
  \label{eq:tanh}
\end{equation}
with transition widths $\delta \sim \mathrm{Uniform}[15\secunit, 60\secunit]$,
consistent with observed current sheet thicknesses at 1 AU \citep{Vasquez2007}.
Injection times follow a Poisson process at $\sim$4 day$^{-1}$ with a 1-hour
minimum separation, consistent with flux-tube boundary crossing rates near
1 AU \citep{Wilcox1965}.

Kolmogorov turbulence ($P(f) \propto |f|^{-5/3}$) is superimposed with
amplitude $\sigma_\mathrm{turb} = \sigma_\mathrm{frac} \times B_0$ per
component.
The standard benchmark uses $\sigma_\mathrm{frac} = 0.30$, consistent with
$\delta B / B \sim 0.2$--$0.4$ at 1 AU \citep{Bruno2013}.
The SNR sweep varies $\sigma_\mathrm{frac}$ from 0.05 to 0.50.
True crossing centers are the exact tanh midpoints and serve as ground truth.
The single-regime benchmark and component ablation use seed 42.
The SNR sweep reports mean $\pm$ std across 5 independent seeds (42--46),
each generating a distinct 7-day realization with 28 injected crossings.

\subsection{Comparison Detectors}
\label{subsec:comparators}

\textbf{PVI.}
The Partial Variance of Increments \citep{Greco2008}:
\begin{equation}
  \PVI(t) \;=\; \frac{|\dBvec(t,\tau)|}{\sqrt{\langle |\dBvec|^2 \rangle_T}},
  \label{eq:pvi}
\end{equation}
with lag $\tau = 1$ sample (3 s), 90-s normalization window, threshold
$\PVI > 3.0$, minimum separation 300 s.

\textbf{$d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$ baseline.}
Threshold at $\mu + 2.5\sigma$ of:
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{score}(t) \;=\; \left|\frac{d\,|\mathbf{B}|}{dt}\right|,
  \label{eq:baseline}
\end{equation}
with 300-s minimum separation.
Note that $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$ measures the scalar magnitude derivative; a clean
polarity reversal can rotate $\mathbf{B}$ by $180^\circ$ while leaving
$|\mathbf{B}|$ nearly constant.
The threshold is set globally from signal statistics, making it sensitive to
the turbulence level (Section~\ref{subsec:snr}).

\textbf{PELT.}
Pruned Exact Linear Time \citep{Killick2012} with $L^2$ (Gaussian
mean-change) cost and BIC penalty $\beta = \log(N)\,\hat{\sigma}^2$, where
$\hat{\sigma}$ is the median-absolute-deviation noise estimate.
Evaluated at penalty scales 1$\times$--1{,}000$\times$ BIC; results reported
in Section~\ref{subsec:snr}.

\subsection{Evaluation Metrics}
\label{subsec:metrics}

A detected event at $\hat{\tau}$ is a true positive (TP) if within tolerance
of an injected crossing center; each crossing is matched at most once.
We report precision $P = \mathrm{TP}/N_\mathrm{det}$, recall
$R = \mathrm{TP}/N_\mathrm{true}$, F1, and FDF $= 1 - P$.
Primary tolerance: 150 s (50 samples at 3-s cadence); strict: 30 s.

For the contaminated benchmark we additionally report the \emph{confounder hit
rate}: the fraction of known non-antipodal events whose location is within
150 s of a detection.

\subsection{Rotation Angle Analysis}
\label{subsec:rotation_method}

For each detection at sample $p$, we compute mean field directions in pre-
and post-windows of $w_a$ seconds:
\begin{align}
  \Bhat_\mathrm{before} &=
    \frac{\langle\Bvec\rangle_{[p-w_a:p]}}{|\langle\Bvec\rangle_{[p-w_a:p]}|},
  \quad
  \Bhat_\mathrm{after} \;=\;
    \frac{\langle\Bvec\rangle_{[p:p+w_a]}}{|\langle\Bvec\rangle_{[p:p+w_a]}|},
  \label{eq:Bhat} \\
  \omega &= \arccos\!\left(\mathrm{clip}(\Bhat_\mathrm{before}
    \cdot \Bhat_\mathrm{after}, -1, 1)\right),
  \label{eq:omega}
\end{align}
with $w_a \in \{10, 30, 60, 120\}\secunit$.
Phase-randomized surrogates (100 realizations) provide a spectral null model.

\textbf{MASS/SMASH precision rises with turbulence.}
The mean precision increases monotonically from $0.853\pm0.099$ at 5\%
turbulence (SNR 20:1) to $0.937\pm0.047$ at 50\% (SNR 2:1).
This counterintuitive result---increasing selectivity in a noisier
environment---reflects the absolute MDL threshold: as turbulence grows, the
noise residuals of both genuine sign flips and spurious candidates grow, but
only genuine sign flips provide a \emph{systematic} residual reduction;
the absolute 20-bit bar filters the borderline cases more aggressively.
At the lowest turbulence level, some low-amplitude structural irregularities
that fall near the 20-bit threshold are accepted; at the highest turbulence
level, only those crossings with sufficiently large polarity contrast relative
to local noise clear the bar, coincidentally eliminating the borderline
candidates.

\textbf{Baseline precision collapses monotonically above 20\% turbulence.}
At 20\%, the baseline produces on average 7 false detections (mean P = 0.807).
At 30\%, this grows to 190 false detections (mean P = 0.129).
At 50\%, it produces 771 false detections (mean P = 0.035).
The collapse occurs because the detection threshold $\mu + 2.5\sigma$ is
computed from the global distribution of $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$, which develops
increasingly heavy tails with rising turbulence amplitude.

\textbf{PVI precision is stable at 0.967--0.968 across all levels.}
PVI's partial stability arises from local variance normalization:
$\PVI(t) = |\dBvec(t,\tau)| / \sqrt{\langle|\dBvec|^2\rangle_T}$.
As turbulence increases, both numerator and denominator increase, partially
compensating each other.
The normalization provides a residual FDF of $\approx 3\%$ at all levels.

\textbf{MASS/SMASH mean recall plateaus at $0.550\pm0.110$ above 20\%
turbulence.}
The consistently detected subset are the ``strong'' crossings: those with
sufficient polarity contrast to earn a 20-bit MDL gain at every noise level.
The missed crossings are consistently the same ``weak'' events: those
embedded in high-variance intervals where MDL gain remains below threshold
regardless of turbulence.
This stable partition---rather than a continuously shifting detection set---is
informative: calibrating the gain threshold against a held-out labeled sample
produces a stable operating point rather than a moving target.

\textbf{At high turbulence, MASS/SMASH F1 dominates the baseline.}
At 30\%: MASS/SMASH F1 = 0.685 (mean) vs baseline 0.227 (mean).
At 50\%: MASS/SMASH F1 = 0.703 (mean) vs baseline 0.068 (mean).
In regimes where noise amplitude is comparable to signal amplitude,
MASS/SMASH is the only single-pass method in this benchmark that produces
a reliable catalog.

\textbf{PELT with standard BIC penalty produces no usable catalog.}
PELT \citep{Killick2012} with $L^2$ cost and BIC penalty
$\beta = \log(N)\hat{\sigma}^2$ produces 1{,}660--1{,}665 detections per
experiment (for 28 true events), yielding $P \approx 0.017$ and $R = 1.000$
at \emph{every} turbulence level 5\%--50\%.
The breakpoint count is essentially constant across the entire turbulence
range: PELT exhibits no turbulence-dependent precision behavior.
This occurs because Kolmogorov turbulence at 3-s cadence produces thousands
of short-duration local mean offsets that collectively overwhelm the BIC
penalty, irrespective of overall turbulence amplitude.
Even at 1{,}000$\times$ the BIC penalty, precision improves only to
$P = 0.084$ (332 detections for 28 true events).
The contrast with MASS/SMASH is stark: an absolute 20-bit MDL threshold
is immune to the penalty-overwhelm mechanism that renders PELT unusable
here, because it does not scale with signal-derived statistics.

\subsection{Contaminated Benchmark: Rejection of Non-Antipodal Events}
\label{subsec:contaminated}

The contaminated benchmark tests precision when non-antipodal confounding
events are present alongside true crossings.
We inject 40 confounders into a 10-day synthetic signal (40 true crossings,
30\% turbulence) at positions well-separated ($> 600\secunit$) from true
crossings.
Two confounder types:

\textbf{Type A --- Variance bursts.}
Gaussian-Hanning noise added to all three $B$ components (amplitude 4 nT,
width 60 s).
This creates a large local $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$ spike and a large $|\dBvec|$,
but random phase structure so $\mathcal{A}(\tau) \approx 0$.

\textbf{Type B --- $B_z$ magnitude dips.}
$B_z$ is scaled to 10\% of its local value via a Gaussian notch
($\sigma \approx 45\secunit$) and then recovers.
This produces two large gradient events (entry and exit) but $B_z$ remains
positive throughout---no sign flip, $\mathcal{A}(\tau) \approx 0$.

\textbf{The baseline fires on 98\% of confounders (39/40).}
Any event with large $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$, regardless of sign-flip structure,
is accepted.
Notably, the baseline's apparent precision on true crossings \emph{improves}
from 0.370 (clean) to 0.506 (contaminated): variance bursts inflate the
global $\sigma$ of $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$, raising the threshold and incidentally
suppressing some turbulent false positives.
This statistical artifact underscores the instability of relative thresholds.

\textbf{MASS/SMASH fires on 18\% of confounders (7/40).}
The antipodal pre-filter rejects most confounders because variance bursts and
magnitude dips do not produce $\mathcal{A}(\tau) > 0.35$.
All 7 confounder hits arise from the 20 variance bursts; none of the 20
$B_z$ dips triggered a MASS/SMASH detection because a positive-valued dip
has zero antipodal correlation.

\textbf{PVI fires on 8\% of confounders (3/40).}
PVI's local variance normalization provides complementary protection:
the normalization partially suppresses isolated bursts, and the 3D vector
nature of PVI dilutes $B_z$-only dips across components.
MASS/SMASH and PVI reject different confounder sub-populations, suggesting
that a joint pipeline would suppress nearly all tested confounder types.

\subsection{Detection Localization Offset}
\label{subsec:offset}

The drop from recall 0.483 (150-s tolerance) to 0.150 (30-s tolerance) in
Table~\ref{tab:main_results} indicates a systematic offset between MASS/SMASH
detections and the injected crossing centers.
The displacement $\Delta\tau = \tau_\mathrm{MS} - \tau_\mathrm{inj}$ clusters
near $\pm 10$ samples ($\pm 30\secunit$) with a spread up to $\pm 50$ samples
($\pm 150\secunit$).

The tanh model has its inflection point at the crossing center, so the offset
does not arise from a shift to a sub-feature of the transition profile.
It reflects: (1) finite chunk step size (300 s between starts),
(2) MDL scoring that maximizes compressibility gain rather than antisymmetry
score, and (3) the forward-ordered correlation formula, which does not
guarantee detection at the midpoint for wide transitions.
The localization precision ($\approx \pm 30\secunit$) is $\approx 0.14\%$ of
the typical inter-event interval ($\sim$6 hours), which is acceptable for
catalog construction.
For applications requiring transition-center accuracy, a post-detection
tanh profile fit recovers sub-10-s localization.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}
%% ============================================================

\subsection{The Precision-Recall Trade-Off}

The SNR sweep makes explicit a precision-recall trade-off that is usually
implicit.
MASS/SMASH accepts mean recall of 39\%--57\% (across 5 seeds) in exchange for
high precision across the full turbulence range---precision that
\emph{rises} from 0.853 to 0.937 as noise increases, while the baseline
precision falls from 1.000 to 0.035 over the same range.

Which side of the trade-off is preferable depends on the application.
For discovery surveys where cataloging every possible event is the priority,
PVI provides better recall at comparable precision.
For follow-up studies where the catalog feeds expensive analysis---reconnection
jet identification, seizure classification, fault diagnosis---a high-precision
catalog of fewer events is more useful than a 3\% precision catalog of many.

\subsection{Why Precision Rises with Turbulence}

The finding that MASS/SMASH mean precision increases monotonically with
turbulence amplitude warrants explanation.
At low turbulence (5\%, SNR 20:1), the MDL gain landscape is relatively flat:
many candidates accumulate modest gain and some low-amplitude non-sign-flip
structures fall near the 20-bit threshold from below, occasionally crossing
it.
At high turbulence (50\%, SNR 2:1), the dominant signal structures are strong
sign flips (large polarity contrast relative to background); turbulent
fluctuations produce chaotic gain distributions that rarely provide the
systematic two-sided residual reduction a genuine sign flip does.
The 20-bit absolute threshold thus acts as a more effective filter against the
ambient noise floor at high turbulence than at low.

This mechanism is complementary to the baseline's failure: as turbulence
grows, the baseline threshold $\mu + 2.5\sigma$ rises but the signal also
rises---eventually the threshold admits thousands of spurious detections.
MASS/SMASH's absolute threshold, denominated in bits, rises automatically with
the signal level (since large-amplitude sign flips earn more bits) and does
not chase the noise floor.

\subsection{Precision Stability vs.\ Bifurcation}

A subtlety in the SNR sweep is that MASS/SMASH recall does not degrade
continuously with turbulence; it plateaus at $0.550\pm0.110$ from
20\%--50\% turbulence, consistently finding the same strong crossings and
missing the same weak ones across all 5 seeds.
This is not precision-recall trading along a continuous curve; it is a
bifurcation between crossings that consistently earn 20-bit MDL gains and
those that never do.

This behavioral stability has a practical implication: calibrating the MDL
gain threshold against a held-out labeled sample produces a stable operating
point rather than a continuous threshold-tuning exercise.
At a lower gain threshold (e.g., 5 bits), recall would increase but precision
stability would likely weaken as turbulence-driven spurious gains become more
frequent.

\subsection{Complementarity and Pipeline Design}

The three detectors occupy distinct precision-recall operating points:
MASS/SMASH (high and rising precision, moderate recall), PVI (very high
precision, moderate recall, better confounder rejection for variance bursts),
and baseline (perfect recall, turbulence-sensitive precision).
A practical three-stage pipeline exploiting complementarity:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Apply PVI to obtain a high-precision ($> 98\%$) initial catalog of
    strong gradient events---the established domain standard.
  \item Apply MASS/SMASH at the 20-bit gain threshold to supplement with
    sign-flip events that PVI misses.
    In the single-regime benchmark at 30\% turbulence, this adds 24 true
    events (at 7.9\% FDF for the MASS/SMASH component).
  \item Use the MDL gain score as a continuous quality ranking for downstream
    prioritization.
\end{enumerate}

The contaminated benchmark result (MASS/SMASH 18\% vs PVI 8\% confounder rate)
suggests that for datasets with many non-antipodal confounders, PVI should be
preferred as the primary detector with MASS/SMASH as supplementary selector.

\subsection{Localization and Post-Processing}

The $\pm 30\secunit$ localization offset is a correctable implementation
artifact.
For applications requiring the transition center, a post-detection tanh
profile fit recovers sub-10-s accuracy.
The MDL gain score provides a natural quality indicator for prioritizing
which detections warrant this refinement.

\subsection{Limitations}
\label{subsec:limits}

\textbf{Quantitative results are from synthetic data; real-data pilot is
descriptive only.}
All P/R/F1 figures come from controlled synthetic experiments.
The 20-day Wind/MFI pilot (Section~\ref{sec:real_data}) confirms qualitatively
consistent behavior but cannot provide precision or recall without a labeled
reference catalog.
Real solar wind current sheets exhibit a wider range of transition profiles,
background orientations, and co-rotating interaction regions not modeled here.
Quantitative validation against published sector-boundary catalogs is required
before performance claims extend to real observations.

\textbf{Single-component detection.}
MASS/SMASH processes only $B_z$.
The benchmark geometry ($\approx 96\%$ reversal amplitude in $B_z$) is
favorable for single-component detection.
A benchmark with randomized field orientations would test oblique crossings;
full 3-component MDL detection would improve recall for those cases.

\textbf{Comparator strength.}
The scalar derivative $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$ is a deliberately simple baseline.
A vector-gradient baseline $|d\mathbf{B}/dt|$ would be stronger, particularly
for polarity reversals where $|\mathbf{B}|$ changes little.
The precision-collapse results reflect the scalar derivative; a vector-gradient
baseline would represent a more demanding test of MASS/SMASH's precision
advantage.
PELT (Gaussian mean-change, BIC penalty) is evaluated in
Section~\ref{subsec:snr} and found to produce more than 1{,}600 detections
regardless of turbulence level; a direction-aware or sign-flip-specific
cost function would be a stronger comparator but would partially replicate
the MASS/SMASH approach.

\textbf{Limited SNR-sweep sample size and geometry.}
Table~\ref{tab:snr_sweep} reports mean $\pm$ std across 5 independent seeds
[42--46], providing calibrated confidence intervals for P, R, and F1.
The precision result (mean P = 0.853--0.937) rests on a total of
7 turbulence levels $\times$ 5 seeds = 35 experiments with the same signal
geometry (radial background field, Poisson injection process) and 3-s cadence.
Replication across longer signals, varying event rates, and oblique field
orientations is needed before precision-stability claims extend to arbitrary
signals.
We present this as a benchmark finding, not a certified algorithm property.

\textbf{Parameter calibration.}
The MDL gain threshold (20 bits), seam penalty ($\alpha = 2.0$), antipodal
threshold (0.35), and roughness threshold (0.20) are heuristically set.
Systematic calibration against labeled training data would provide
better-characterized operating points.

\section{Pilot Application to Real Wind/MFI Data}
\label{sec:real_data}
%% ============================================================

To provide a qualitative consistency check beyond synthetic experiments,
we applied all three detectors to 20 days of publicly available Wind spacecraft
Magnetic Fields Investigation (MFI) data, covering 2001-03-01 to 2001-03-20
(dataset WI\_H0\_MFI, variable BGSE, 3-second cadence; NASA CDAWeb archive,
\citealt{Lepping1995}).
This period sits near the peak of Solar Cycle 23, when the heliospheric current
sheet is tilted, warped, and multiply sheeted.
No manually labeled catalog is used; the pilot is descriptive, not
quantitative in P/R/F1 terms.

\textbf{Dataset characteristics.}
The 20-day record contains 572,316 three-second samples (minor gaps removed).
The field magnitude averages $\langle|\mathbf{B}|\rangle = 6.49\nT$ with
standard deviation $3.72\nT$; the $B_z$ component spans $[-20.1, 17.0]\nT$.
The ratio $\sigma_{|\mathbf{B}|} / \langle|\mathbf{B}|\rangle \approx 0.57$
is consistent with turbulence in the 30--50\% range tested in the SNR
sweep---the most demanding synthetic regime.

MASS/SMASH produces 7.8$\times$ fewer detections than the threshold baseline
and 15.6$\times$ fewer than PVI, consistent with the high-turbulence synthetic

The ratio is in close agreement with the multi-seed sweep at equivalent
turbulence: at 30\%--50\%, the baseline produces on average
$\sim$6$\times$--$27\times$ more total detections than MASS/SMASH.

A note on taxonomy is important.
PVI and the baseline respond to gradient magnitude and count a broad family of
intermittent structures; MASS/SMASH counts only $B_z$ \emph{sign-flip}
change-points---a strict structural subset.
The PVI rate of 120 day$^{-1}$ is not implausible as a count of
\emph{general} intermittent structures \citep{Greco2009}.
The MASS/SMASH rate of 7.7 day$^{-1}$ is plausible for sign-flip current
sheets during a high-activity period; without a labeled reference catalog,
we cannot determine what fraction are large-scale sector boundaries versus
smaller sign-flip structures.

\textbf{Qualitative assessment.}
Figure~\ref{fig:real_overview} shows the 20-day $B_z$ record with detections
overlaid.
MASS/SMASH fires predominantly on the large-amplitude, sustained $B_z$
reversals visible by eye; the baseline and PVI also fire densely during broad
turbulent intervals where no sustained polarity reversal is apparent.
This qualitative pattern mirrors the synthetic experiments: the antipodal
pre-filter suppresses events lacking sign-reversal antisymmetry, regardless
of gradient amplitude.

\section{Projective PVI: Magnitude-Invariant Direction-Change Detection}
\label{sec:projpvi}
%% ============================================================

Standard PVI responds to the Euclidean increment $|\Delta\Bvec(t)|$, which
conflates magnitude changes with direction changes.
We introduce \emph{projective PVI} ($\pPVI$), replacing the Euclidean
increment with the geodesic distance in the real projective plane
$\mathbb{RP}^2$ to provide a complementary, magnitude-invariant
characterization of field-direction changes.

\subsection{Formulation}

The real projective plane $\mathbb{RP}^2$ is the space of unoriented
directions in $\mathbb{R}^3$: antipodal unit vectors $\Bhat$ and $-\Bhat$
represent the same element.
The geodesic distance between two normalized field directions is
\begin{equation}
  d_{\mathbb{RP}^2}(\Bhat_1, \Bhat_2)
    = \arccos\!\bigl(\bigl|\Bhat_1 \cdot \Bhat_2\bigr|\bigr)
    \;\in\; \bigl[0,\tfrac{\pi}{2}\bigr].
  \label{eq:projpvi}
\end{equation}
The absolute value on the dot product is the antipodal identification:
directions that differ only by a sign flip are at geodesic distance zero.

Projective PVI normalizes this distance identically to standard PVI:
\begin{equation}
  \pPVI(t) =
  \frac{d_{\mathbb{RP}^2}\!\bigl(\Bhat(t-\tau),\;\Bhat(t)\bigr)}
       {\sqrt{\bigl\langle d_{\mathbb{RP}^2}^{2}\bigr\rangle_{T}}}
\end{equation}
using lag $\tau = 1$ sample and running RMS window $T = 30$ samples.

Three key properties follow directly:
(1) A pure polarity reversal ($\Bvec \to -\Bvec$) gives
$d_{\mathbb{RP}^2} = \arccos(|-1|) = 0$---invisible by construction.
(2) A $90^\circ$ direction rotation gives
$d_{\mathbb{RP}^2} = \arccos(0) = \pi/2$---maximum signal.
(3) A magnitude burst at constant direction gives
$d_{\mathbb{RP}^2} = 0$---invisible.
This last property is the decisive difference from standard PVI: $\pPVI$
responds exclusively to \emph{field direction changes} and is immune to
magnitude fluctuations that dominate solar wind turbulence.

In the synthetic benchmark, polarity-reversal current sheets pass through
near-zero $|\Bvec|$ at the crossing center; near $|\Bvec| \approx 0$, the
normalized direction $\Bhat = \Bvec/|\Bvec|$ is dominated by the turbulent
component and varies rapidly, producing large $d_{\mathbb{RP}^2}$ values at
the transition zone.
Projective PVI therefore detects sign-flip sheets \emph{via their near-null
field region}---physically meaningful, as observed HCS crossings are
frequently associated with a minimum in $|\Bvec|$ \citep{Wilcox1965}.

\subsection{Precision-Recall Complementarity}

The two tables reveal a clean precision-recall duality.
On polarity-reversal events (Table~\ref{tab:projpvi_polarity}), MASS/SMASH
occupies the precision-optimised extreme: $\mathrm{P} = 1.000$ at every
turbulence level (seed 42; multi-seed mean P = 0.853--0.937, Table~\ref{tab:snr_sweep})
with $\mathrm{R} = 0.500$--$0.679$.
Projective PVI occupies the recall-optimised extreme: $\mathrm{R} = 0.964$--$1.000$
at every turbulence level with $\mathrm{P} = 0.730$--$0.824$.
Standard PVI lies between them ($\mathrm{P} = 0.944$--$0.947$,
$\mathrm{R} = 0.607$--$0.643$).

By F1 score, $\pPVI$ outperforms both other methods on the polarity-reversal
benchmark at every turbulence level (F1 = 0.831--0.903 vs.\ 0.667--0.809 for
MASS/SMASH and 0.739--0.766 for standard PVI).

On pure-rotation events (Table~\ref{tab:projpvi_rotation}), the roles reverse.
MASS/SMASH recovers 35.7\%--60.7\% of rotation events non-monotonically:
recall rises from 5\% to 30\% turbulence as the roughness detector generates
more candidates, then falls at 50\% as the MDL gate requires more compression
gain; a $90^\circ$ rotation produces a $B_z$ step of only $6\nT$ vs.\
$12\nT$ for a sign flip, giving $\sim$$4\times$ lower MDL gain that falls
below threshold at high background noise.
Standard PVI and $\pPVI$ recover $\geq 92.9\%$ of rotation events across the
full turbulence range.

The complementarity can be exploited in a two-stage pipeline: $\pPVI$ as a
high-recall first stage (finds all direction-change events, including polarity
reversals via near-null detection), followed by MASS/SMASH as a precision
gate (confirms only sign-flip topology changes).

\textbf{Real Wind/MFI data.}
On the 20-day real dataset, $\pPVI$ produces $2{,}524$ detections
($126.2\,\mathrm{day}^{-1}$) compared with $2{,}397$ for standard PVI and
154 for MASS/SMASH.
The near-identical counts of $\pPVI$ and standard PVI reflect that turbulent
fluctuations in real solar wind simultaneously perturb both field magnitude
and direction; the two detectors overlap heavily in the real-data regime.
MASS/SMASH remains $16\times$ more selective than either PVI variant,
consistent with its sign-flip specialization.

%% ============================================================
\section{Potential Broader Applicability}
\label{sec:applications}
%% ============================================================

The two structural requirements for MASS/SMASH are: (1) events of interest are
sign-flip change-points embedded in broadband noise, and (2) false detections
are more costly than missed detections.
We identify three domains where these conditions plausibly hold.
These are hypotheses, not results; transfer requires parameter recalibration
and domain-specific evaluation.

\textbf{Magnetospheric current sheets.}
Substorm-driven current sheet flapping in the magnetospheric tail produces
$B_z$ polarity reversals at MMS and Cluster spacecraft
\citep{Chasapis2015,Retino2007} with signal structure identical to solar wind
current sheets but at kinetic spatial scales and steeper turbulence spectra.
Window and step sizes would need recalibration to shorter correlation times.

\textbf{EEG reference electrode inversions.}
Bipolar EEG inversions produce clean sign flips in individual channels.
Heuristic amplitude thresholds generate many false positives during the
high-amplitude pre-ictal interval \citep{Shoeb2004}---a precision-collapse
setting where the antipodal pre-filter might select structural inversions over
unstructured noise bursts.

\textbf{Climate polarity transitions.}
ENSO transitions (El Ni\~no $\to$ La Ni\~na) are sign flips in the Ni\~no-3.4
index embedded in interannual variability \citep{Trenberth1990}.
With $N \sim 10^2$--$10^3$ monthly values, chunked inference would require
much smaller windows and threshold recalibration from labeled training data.

%% ============================================================
\section{Conclusions}
\label{sec:conclusions}
%% ============================================================

We have introduced MASS/SMASH, an MDL-regularized algorithm for detecting
sign-flip change-points---polarity reversals from $+\mu_0$ to $-\mu_0$---in
noisy univariate time series.
The algorithm combines an antipodal correlation pre-filter, a roughness
discontinuity detector, and an absolute MDL gain acceptance criterion.
Three solar wind synthetic experiments and one real-data pilot yield the
following principal findings.

\begin{enumerate}

\item \textbf{MASS/SMASH precision rises with turbulence while the baseline
collapses.}
Across 5 independent seeds and 7 turbulence levels (5\%--50\%, SNR 20:1 to
2:1), MASS/SMASH mean precision rises monotonically from $0.853\pm0.099$ at
5\% turbulence to $0.937\pm0.047$ at 50\%---increasing selectivity as noise
grows.
Over the same range, the $d|\mathbf{B}|/dt$ threshold baseline falls from
precision 1.000 to 0.035 (771 spurious detections for 28 true events at
50\%).
PVI precision is stable at 0.967--0.968 via local variance normalization.
The rising MASS/SMASH precision reflects the absolute MDL threshold becoming
increasingly effective at filtering borderline candidates as turbulence grows.

\item \textbf{MASS/SMASH recall bifurcates rather than degrades continuously.}
Recall plateaus at $0.550\pm0.110$ above 20\% turbulence as the population
partitions into a consistent strong subset (always detected) and a consistent
weak subset (never detected at 20-bit threshold).
This stable partition is more useful for follow-up studies than a threshold
whose detection set shifts unpredictably with noise.

\item \textbf{PELT with BIC penalty collapses to $P = 0.017$ regardless of
turbulence.}
PELT \citep{Killick2012} with Gaussian mean-change cost and BIC penalty
produces 1{,}660--1{,}665 detections for 28 true events at every turbulence
level 5\%--50\%.
Even at 1{,}000$\times$ BIC, precision reaches only 0.084.
Signal-derived penalties are overwhelmed by Kolmogorov turbulence; the
absolute 20-bit MDL gate is necessary for precision stability.

\item \textbf{Confounder rejection: 18\% fire rate for MASS/SMASH vs 98\%
for the baseline.}
When 40 non-antipodal confounders are injected alongside 40 true crossings,
the baseline fires on 98\%, MASS/SMASH on 18\%, and PVI on 8\%.
MASS/SMASH and PVI have complementary rejection mechanisms.

\item \textbf{Complementarity with PVI: joint catalog covers 95\% of events
at $\approx$5\% combined FDF.}
Of the 58 single-regime benchmark true positives, 24 correspond to crossings
PVI missed.
A PVI-first, MASS/SMASH-supplement pipeline provides the broadest coverage
with manageable false discovery fraction.

\item \textbf{Component ablation: MDL gate = precision stabilizer;
roughness detector = primary recall driver; antipodal pre-filter =
confounder rejector.}
Antipodal-only produces 1,316 detections at precision 0.014 at every
turbulence level.
All three MDL-gated variants maintain precision 1.000 for the single-seed
ablation.
Roughness+MDL recall matches Full MASS/SMASH recall at every turbulence
level $\geq 10\%$; the antipodal pre-filter contributes marginal recall only
at 5\% turbulence and structural selectivity (confounder rejection) throughout.

\item \textbf{Rotation angle analysis: MASS/SMASH true positives are
indistinguishable from PVI events at matched positions.}
The $\approx \pm 30\secunit$ localization offset is an implementation artifact
of finite chunk discretization.
At matched injected positions, MASS/SMASH and PVI mean rotation angles agree
to within $1^\circ$--$14^\circ$ across all averaging windows.

\item \textbf{Qualitative consistency on real Wind/MFI data.}
On 20 days of real Wind/MFI data (March 2001), MASS/SMASH produces
154 detections (7.7 day$^{-1}$)---7.8$\times$ fewer than the baseline and
15.6$\times$ fewer than PVI---matching the high-turbulence synthetic pattern.

\item \textbf{Projective PVI complements MASS/SMASH across event classes.}
Projective PVI ($\pPVI$), using geodesic distance in $\mathbb{RP}^2$, achieves
$\mathrm{R} \approx 1.000$ on polarity-reversal events at
$\mathrm{P} = 0.730$--$0.824$---the recall-optimised pole of the
precision-recall trade-off.
On pure-rotation events (no polarity flip), $\pPVI$ and standard PVI maintain
$\mathrm{R} \geq 0.929$ while MASS/SMASH recovers 36\%--61\% non-monotonically.
A $\pPVI$-first, MASS/SMASH-confirmation pipeline provides high-recall
discovery with precision-stable confirmation.

\end{enumerate}

Future work will address: (a) quantitative validation against labeled
sector-boundary catalogs from Parker Solar Probe FIELDS, Solar Orbiter MAG,
and Wind/MFI with expert annotation; (b) multi-component and vector-magnitude
detection for oblique crossings; (c) broader multi-geometry replication across
non-radial and oblique field orientations (the present multi-seed sweep uses
a purely radial synthetic geometry); (d) systematic MDL gain threshold
calibration against labeled training data; (e) auto-recentering via
post-detection tanh profile fit to resolve the $\pm 30\secunit$ localization
offset; (f) prospective validation in magnetospheric and EEG domains with
appropriate recalibration; and (g) comparison of projective PVI catalogs with
published rotational and tangential discontinuity catalogs to characterize how
the two PVI variants partition real solar wind discontinuity populations.
